Example Of A Push Day Tips And Tricks For A Successful Workout

If you're looking to build strength and muscle in your upper body, a push day should be a staple in your workout routine. In this article, we'll go over what a push day is, what exercises you can include, and how to structure your workout for maximum effectiveness.

What is a Push Day?

A push day is a workout focused on exercises that involve pushing movements for the upper body. This includes exercises like bench press, overhead press, and push-ups. The goal of a push day is to build strength and muscle in the chest, shoulders, and triceps.

Exercises for Push Day

Here are some exercises you can include in your push day workout:
  • Bench press
  • Incline bench press
  • Overhead press
  • Push-ups
  • Dumbbell flyes
  • Dumbbell shoulder press
  • Tricep dips
  • Tricep pushdowns

Structuring Your Workout

To get the most out of your push day workout, it's important to structure it properly. Here's an example of how you can structure your push day workout:
  1. Warm-up: 5-10 minutes of light cardio and stretching
  2. Main lifts: 3-4 sets of 6-8 reps of bench press, overhead press, and/or incline bench press
  3. Accessory lifts: 3-4 sets of 8-12 reps of dumbbell flyes, dumbbell shoulder press, and/or tricep dips
  4. Finisher: 2-3 sets of push-ups to failure
  5. Cool-down: 5-10 minutes of stretching

Tips for a Successful Push Day

Here are some tips to help you have a successful push day:
  • Focus on proper form: It's important to use proper form during your exercises to avoid injury and get the most out of your workout.
  • Progressive overload: To continue seeing progress, you need to gradually increase the weight or reps you're doing over time.
  • Rest between sets: Take 1-2 minutes of rest between sets to allow your muscles to recover.
  • Stay hydrated: Drink water throughout your workout to avoid dehydration.
  • Listen to your body: If something doesn't feel right, stop and adjust your form or weight.

Common FAQs About Push Day

How often should I do a push day?

It's generally recommended to do a push day once or twice a week, depending on your fitness goals and schedule.

What should I eat before and after my push day workout?

Before your workout, aim for a meal or snack that's high in carbs and protein for energy. After your workout, aim for a meal or snack that's high in protein to help with muscle recovery.

Pros and Cons of Push Day

Pros

  • Builds strength and muscle in the upper body
  • Can improve posture and overall upper body function
  • Can help with weight loss and maintenance

Cons

  • Can put strain on the shoulder and elbow joints if not done with proper form
  • May not be suitable for those with certain injuries or health conditions
  • May not be as effective for overall muscle and strength gains as a more comprehensive workout routine
Overall, a push day can be a great addition to your workout routine if done properly and with proper form. By including exercises like bench press, overhead press, and push-ups, you can build strength and muscle in your upper body and improve your overall function and posture.
