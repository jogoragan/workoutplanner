What To Do In Boston On Monday A Guide To Enjoying The City

Welcome to Boston, where history meets modernity. Whether you're a local or a tourist, we've got you covered for what to do in Boston on Monday. From museums to food to outdoor activities, Boston has it all. Here's our guide to help you make the most of your day in this beautiful city.

  • Food

Museums

Boston is home to some of the most famous museums in the world. On a Monday, you can visit these museums without the crowds that usually come on weekends. Here are some of our top picks:

The Museum of Fine Arts

The Museum of Fine Arts is one of the largest art museums in the world. It has over 450,000 works of art, including paintings, sculptures, and textiles. The museum is open from 10am to 5pm on Mondays. General admission is $25 for adults.

The Isabella Stewart Gardner Museum

The Isabella Stewart Gardner Museum is a stunning art museum that was founded by Isabella Stewart Gardner in 1903. The museum is located in a beautiful building that was designed to look like a Venetian palace. The museum is open from 11am to 5pm on Mondays. General admission is $20 for adults.

Food

Boston is known for its delicious seafood, but it also has a variety of other cuisines to offer. Here are some of our top picks for where to eat on a Monday:

The Barking Crab

The Barking Crab is a waterfront seafood spot that has been a Boston staple for over 20 years. They serve fresh seafood, including lobster, clams, and oysters. The restaurant is open from 11:30am to 9pm on Mondays.

Toro

Toro is a tapas restaurant that serves small plates inspired by Spanish cuisine. They have a great selection of wines and cocktails to pair with your meal. The restaurant is open from 5pm to 10pm on Mondays.

Outdoor Activities

If you're looking to enjoy the outdoors on a Monday, Boston has plenty of options. Here are some of our top picks:

The Boston Public Garden

The Boston Public Garden is a beautiful park that is located in the heart of the city. It's the perfect place to go for a stroll or have a picnic. You can also take a ride on the famous Swan Boats. The park is open from dawn until dusk.

The Freedom Trail

The Freedom Trail is a 2.5-mile walking trail that takes you through 16 historical sites in Boston. You can follow the trail on your own or take a guided tour. The trail is open from dawn until dusk.

Shopping

Boston has some great shopping options, from high-end boutiques to vintage shops. Here are some of our top picks:

Newbury Street

Newbury Street is a popular shopping destination that has a mix of high-end boutiques and chain stores. You can find everything from designer clothing to unique gifts. The stores are open from 11am to 7pm on Mondays.

The SoWa Open Market

The SoWa Open Market is a weekly outdoor market that features local artisans and food vendors. You can find everything from handmade jewelry to gourmet food. The market is open from 11am to 5pm on Mondays.

Nightlife

Boston has a vibrant nightlife scene, with everything from dive bars to rooftop lounges. Here are some of our top picks:

The Beehive

The Beehive is a jazz bar that also serves delicious food. They have live music every night of the week. The bar is open from 5pm to 12am on Mondays.

The Lookout Rooftop and Bar

The Lookout Rooftop and Bar is located on the 9th floor of the Envoy Hotel. It has stunning views of the Boston skyline and serves creative cocktails. The bar is open from 4pm to 10pm on Mondays.

Conclusion

Whether you're looking for history, food, or nightlife, Boston has something for everyone on a Monday. We hope our guide has given you some ideas for how to enjoy this beautiful city. Don't forget to wear comfortable shoes and dress for the weather, as Boston can be unpredictable. Have fun exploring!
