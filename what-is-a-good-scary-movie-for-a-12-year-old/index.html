Welcome to our guide on what is a good scary movie for a 12 year old. As a parent, you may be wondering what movies are appropriate for your child to watch. It's important to find a balance between entertainment and age-appropriate content. In this article, we will provide you with tips and recommendations on how to find the perfect scary movie for your 12 year old.

How to Choose a Scary Movie for a 12 Year Old

Choosing a scary movie for your 12 year old can be a daunting task. You want to find a movie that will scare them, but not give them nightmares. Here are some tips on how to choose a good scary movie for a 12 year old:

Consider Your Child's Sensitivity to Scary Movies

Every child is different when it comes to their sensitivity to scary movies. Some children may be scared by movies that others find tame. Take into consideration your child's past experiences with scary movies and how they reacted to them. If your child is easily scared, it's best to choose a movie with a lower scare factor.

Check Movie Ratings

Movie ratings are a great way to determine if a movie is appropriate for your child. Look for movies with a PG or PG-13 rating. These movies are usually suitable for children and have less graphic content than R-rated movies.

Read Reviews

Reading reviews from other parents and movie critics can give you a better idea of what to expect from a movie. Look for reviews that mention the movie's scare factor and if it's appropriate for children.

Top Scary Movies for 12 Year Olds

Here are some top scary movies for 12 year olds:

Goosebumps (2015)

Goosebumps is a PG-rated movie that is based on the popular children's book series by R.L. Stine. The movie follows a group of teenagers who must save their town from monsters that have been released from Stine's books.

Coraline (2009)

Coraline is a PG-rated movie that follows a young girl who discovers a hidden door in her new home that leads to an alternate world. The world seems perfect at first, but she soon realizes that it's not what it seems.

The House with a Clock in Its Walls (2018)

The House with a Clock in Its Walls is a PG-rated movie that follows a young orphan who goes to live with his uncle in a mysterious house. He soon discovers that his uncle is a warlock and that the house has a dangerous secret.

Monster House (2006)

Monster House is a PG-rated movie that follows three friends who discover that a spooky house in their neighborhood is actually alive. They must stop the house from eating trick-or-treaters on Halloween.

Tips for Watching Scary Movies with Your Child

Watching scary movies with your child can be a fun bonding experience. Here are some tips to make the experience more enjoyable:

Watch the Movie First

Before watching a scary movie with your child, it's a good idea to watch it first. This will give you an idea of what to expect and if it's appropriate for your child.

Watch During the Daytime

Watching a scary movie during the daytime can make it less scary for your child. They will feel more comfortable knowing that it's still light outside.

Discuss the Movie Afterwards

After watching the movie, take some time to discuss it with your child. Talk about the parts that scared them and how they would have reacted in the same situation. This can help them process their emotions and make the experience less scary.

FAQs

What is the Best Scary Movie for a 12 Year Old?

The best scary movie for a 12 year old depends on their sensitivity to scary movies. Some good options include Goosebumps, Coraline, The House with a Clock in Its Walls, and Monster House.

What Should I Consider When Choosing a Scary Movie for My 12 Year Old?

When choosing a scary movie for your 12 year old, consider their sensitivity to scary movies, movie ratings, and reviews from other parents and movie critics.

Pros and Cons of Watching Scary Movies

Pros

  • Scary movies can be fun and entertaining.
  • Watching scary movies can help children face their fears.
  • Scary movies can be a bonding experience for families.

Cons

  • Scary movies can be too intense for some children.
  • Watching scary movies can cause nightmares and anxiety.
  • Scary movies can desensitize children to violence and gore.

Now that you know what to look for in a good scary movie for a 12 year old, you can choose a movie that will scare your child without giving them nightmares. Remember to always consider your child's sensitivity to scary movies and watch the movie first before watching it with them. Happy watching!
