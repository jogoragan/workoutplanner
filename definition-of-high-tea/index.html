Definition of High Tea

High tea is a British tradition that involves serving a light meal in the late afternoon or early evening. The meal typically includes tea, sandwiches, scones, and cakes or pastries. High tea is often confused with afternoon tea, but the two are actually quite different. Afternoon tea is a more formal affair that involves serving tea, sandwiches, scones, and pastries on a tiered stand. High tea, on the other hand, is a heartier meal that is served at a higher table, hence the name "high" tea.

History of High Tea

The origins of high tea can be traced back to the 18th century when the working class would return home from work and have a light meal of tea and bread. This meal was known as "low" tea because it was eaten at a low table. In the 19th century, the upper class began to adopt this tradition, but they would have their tea and meal at a higher table, which became known as "high" tea. High tea eventually became a popular social event, with people gathering to enjoy tea, sandwiches, and other treats in the late afternoon or early evening.

High Tea Customs

When attending a high tea event, there are a few customs to keep in mind. First, always dress appropriately. High tea is a more formal occasion, so dress in business casual or formal attire. Second, wait for the host or hostess to pour the tea before pouring your own. Third, use the correct utensils when eating. Sandwiches should be eaten with your hands, while scones and pastries should be eaten with a fork and knife. Lastly, always say "thank you" to the host or hostess for their hospitality.

Hosting Your Own High Tea

If you want to host your own high tea gathering, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, decide on a menu. Your menu should include tea, sandwiches, scones, and cakes or pastries. Second, set the table. Use a higher table and decorate it with a tablecloth, flowers, and tea cups and saucers. Third, prepare the food. Make sure to have a variety of sandwiches, such as cucumber and cream cheese, egg salad, and ham and cheese. Bake fresh scones and serve them with clotted cream and jam. Lastly, don't forget the tea! Offer a variety of teas, such as black, green, and herbal, and make sure to have milk, sugar, and lemon available.

Tips for a Successful High Tea

Hosting a successful high tea gathering takes some planning and preparation. Here are a few tips to make your event a success:

  • Invite guests at least two weeks in advance
  • Offer a variety of sandwiches and pastries
  • Prepare the food the day before the event
  • Use a tiered stand to display the food
  • Offer a variety of teas and accompaniments

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What's the difference between high tea and afternoon tea?

A: High tea is a heartier meal that is served at a higher table, while afternoon tea is a more formal affair that involves serving tea, sandwiches, scones, and pastries on a tiered stand.

Q: Do I have to dress up for high tea?

A: Yes, high tea is a more formal occasion, so dress in business casual or formal attire.

Q: What kind of tea should I serve?

A: Offer a variety of teas, such as black, green, and herbal, and make sure to have milk, sugar, and lemon available.

Pros and Cons of High Tea

Like any tradition, there are pros and cons to high tea. Here are a few to consider:

Pros

  • High tea is a fun and unique way to socialize with friends and family
  • The food is delicious and can be customized to your liking
  • High tea is a great way to experience British culture

Cons

  • High tea can be expensive, especially if you go to a fancy restaurant or hotel
  • Hosting a high tea gathering takes some planning and preparation
  • High tea can be a bit stuffy and formal for some people's taste

In conclusion, high tea is a beloved British tradition that involves serving a light meal in the late afternoon or early evening. Whether you attend a high tea event or host your own, it's a fun and unique way to socialize with friends and family while enjoying delicious food and tea.
