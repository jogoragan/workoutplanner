How Many Hours Do School Bus Drivers Work?

School bus drivers typically work between 4-6 hours a day, depending on the school district and the route they are assigned. However, this does not include the time spent preparing the bus before and after their shift. Some drivers may also work split shifts, meaning they work early in the morning and then again in the afternoon.

In addition to driving the bus, school bus drivers are responsible for ensuring the safety of the students on board. They must maintain order and discipline among the students, and report any incidents that occur during their shift. They also perform routine maintenance on the bus and keep it clean and in good working order.

What Does a School Bus Driver Do?

As mentioned, school bus drivers are responsible for transporting students to and from school safely. This involves driving the bus, monitoring students on board, and following a strict schedule. They must also perform pre-trip and post-trip inspections of the bus to ensure it is in good working order.

Other duties of a school bus driver include:

  • Reporting any accidents or incidents that occur during their shift
  • Maintaining order and discipline among the students on board
  • Keeping accurate records of their driving time and mileage
  • Communicating with school administrators, parents, and other drivers

Pros and Cons of Being a School Bus Driver

Pros

One of the biggest advantages of being a school bus driver is the flexibility of the job. Since most drivers work only a few hours a day, they have the rest of the day free to pursue other interests or work another job. Other benefits of the job include:

  • Good pay and benefits
  • Opportunities for overtime and bonuses
  • Job security
  • The chance to interact with children and make a positive impact on their lives

Cons

Like any job, there are also some downsides to being a school bus driver. Some of the cons of the job include:

  • Early morning and afternoon hours
  • Potentially difficult or disruptive students on board
  • Having to deal with traffic and inclement weather conditions
  • The responsibility of ensuring the safety of the students on board

FAQ

Q: Do school bus drivers work weekends?

A: Generally, no. School bus drivers typically only work on weekdays when school is in session.

Q: Do school bus drivers need a special license?

A: Yes. To become a school bus driver, you must have a commercial driver's license (CDL) with a passenger endorsement and a school bus endorsement.

Q: How long does it take to become a school bus driver?

A: The length of time it takes to become a school bus driver varies by state and school district. However, most require at least 6-8 weeks of training before allowing drivers to operate a school bus.

Q: Is being a school bus driver a good job?

A: It depends on your personal preferences and priorities. As mentioned, there are both pros and cons to the job. However, many people find it to be a rewarding and fulfilling career.

Q: How much do school bus drivers make?

A: According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, the median annual wage for bus drivers, including school bus drivers, was $33,920 as of May 2021.

So, now you know how many hours a day school bus drivers work and what their job entails. If you're considering a career as a school bus driver, be sure to weigh the pros and cons carefully before making your decision. Good luck!
