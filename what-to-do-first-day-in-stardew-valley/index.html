Are you new to Stardew Valley and wondering what to do on your first day? Don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we will guide you through the necessary steps to make the most of your first day in Stardew Valley. Here's what we will cover:

Step 1: Meet The Villagers

The first thing you should do is explore the town and meet the villagers. This is important because it will help you build relationships with them, which can lead to various benefits later on. You can find the villagers in their homes, at the town square, or at the beach.

When you meet a villager, talk to them and introduce yourself. They will tell you about themselves and their likes and dislikes. You can also give them gifts to increase their affection towards you. However, make sure to give them something they like, or they might get offended.

Step 2: Clear Your Farm

After you've met the villagers, head to your farm and clear out any debris or weeds. This will give you space to plant your crops and build structures later on. Use your scythe to clear out weeds and your axe to chop down trees and stumps.

You can also collect any forage items you find on your farm or in the surrounding area. These can be used for cooking, gifting, or selling.

Step 3: Plant Seeds

Now that your farm is clear, it's time to start planting your crops. You can buy seeds from Pierre's General Store or JojaMart. Choose the seeds based on the season, as some crops only grow in specific seasons.

Use your hoe to till the soil and then plant the seeds. Make sure to water them every day and keep an eye on their growth. Once they are fully grown, you can harvest them and sell them for a profit.

Step 4: Explore The Mines

If you have some extra time, head to the mines and explore them. You can find valuable ores and gems that can be used for crafting or selling. However, be careful of monsters that lurk in the mines. Bring a weapon and some food to keep your health up.

Step 5: Attend The Egg Festival (If Applicable)

If you start your game in Spring, you may have the opportunity to attend the Egg Festival on your first day. This is a fun event where you can participate in egg hunting and interact with the villagers. Make sure to bring some money to buy items from the festival.

Tips For Your First Day

  • Don't spend all your money on the first day. Save some for future expenses.
  • Check the weather forecast before planting your crops. Rainy days don't require watering.
  • Take breaks and don't exhaust yourself. Your energy level is important.
  • Make a to-do list for the next day to stay organized.

FAQs About Your First Day

Can I skip any of the steps?

Yes, you can skip any of the steps if you don't have enough time or energy. However, it's recommended to at least meet the villagers and clear your farm on the first day.

Do I have to attend the Egg Festival?

No, the Egg Festival is optional and only available in Spring. You can still have a productive first day without attending it.

Pros And Cons Of Your First Day

Pros:

  • You can build relationships with the villagers.
  • You can start planting crops and earning money.
  • You can explore the mines and find valuable items.

Cons:

  • You may not have enough time or energy to do everything.
  • You may make mistakes and waste resources.
  • You may miss out on some events or opportunities.

Overall, your first day in Stardew Valley can be both exciting and overwhelming. Just take it one step at a time and enjoy the experience. Good luck!
