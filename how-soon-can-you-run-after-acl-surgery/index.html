After undergoing ACL surgery, many people are eager to get back to their regular activities, including running. However, returning to running too soon can lead to further injury and setback in recovery. In this article, we will discuss how soon you can run after ACL surgery, how to safely return to running, and some tips to help you get back on track.

How Soon Can You Run After ACL Surgery?

The timeline for returning to running after ACL surgery varies depending on the individual and the type of surgery performed. Generally, it is recommended to wait at least 6 months before returning to running. This allows for proper healing of the ACL graft and surrounding tissues.

However, some people may be able to start running earlier, around 3-4 months post-surgery, if they have met certain criteria. This includes having full range of motion, good muscle strength and control, and clearance from their surgeon and physical therapist.

How to Safely Return to Running After ACL Surgery

Returning to running after ACL surgery should be done gradually and under the guidance of a physical therapist. A structured rehabilitation program should be followed to ensure proper healing and prevent further injury.

The first step in returning to running is to focus on regaining full range of motion and muscle strength. This may include exercises such as quad sets, heel slides, and leg raises. Once this is achieved, the focus shifts to improving balance, coordination, and agility.

Next, a walking program is usually prescribed before starting to run. This involves gradually increasing the amount of time spent walking and the speed at which you walk. Once you are able to walk for 30 minutes without pain or discomfort, you can start incorporating short intervals of running.

Step-by-Step Guide to Returning to Running After ACL Surgery

Here is a general guide to returning to running after ACL surgery:

  1. Focus on regaining full range of motion and muscle strength through exercises prescribed by your physical therapist.
  2. Improve balance, coordination, and agility through specific exercises and drills.
  3. Start a walking program, gradually increasing the time and speed of walking.
  4. Gradually introduce short intervals of running, starting with 30 seconds and building up to 5 minutes.
  5. Slowly increase the amount of time spent running while monitoring for pain or discomfort.
  6. Once you are able to run for 30 minutes without pain or discomfort, you can start incorporating more advanced running drills and training.

Tips for Returning to Running After ACL Surgery

Here are some tips to help you safely return to running after ACL surgery:

  • Follow a structured rehabilitation program prescribed by your physical therapist.
  • Listen to your body and monitor for pain or discomfort.
  • Start with short intervals of running and gradually increase the time and intensity.
  • Wear proper footwear and clothing to prevent injury and discomfort.
  • Stay hydrated and fuel your body with proper nutrition.
  • Don't push yourself too hard too soon, and be patient with the process.

Solutions to Common Problems When Returning to Running After ACL Surgery

Returning to running after ACL surgery can come with some challenges. Here are some solutions to common problems:

  • If you experience pain or discomfort while running, reduce the duration or intensity of your run and focus on rest and recovery.
  • If you have difficulty regaining full range of motion, speak with your physical therapist about specific exercises and modifications to your rehabilitation program.
  • If you are having trouble with balance and coordination, incorporate specific exercises and drills to improve these skills.
  • If you are struggling with motivation or staying on track, consider joining a running group or working with a coach to help keep you accountable.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I run after ACL surgery?

Yes, you can run after ACL surgery, but it is important to follow a structured rehabilitation program and only return to running once you have met certain criteria and have been cleared by your surgeon and physical therapist.

How long after ACL surgery can I start running?

It is generally recommended to wait at least 6 months before returning to running after ACL surgery. However, some people may be able to start running earlier if they have met certain criteria and have been cleared by their surgeon and physical therapist.

What exercises should I do to prepare for running after ACL surgery?

Exercises to prepare for running after ACL surgery may include quad sets, heel slides, leg raises, balance and coordination drills, and agility exercises. It is important to work with a physical therapist to develop a structured rehabilitation program tailored to your individual needs.

Pros and Cons of Returning to Running After ACL Surgery

Pros

  • Improved physical fitness and overall health
  • Increased mental well-being and stress relief
  • Ability to participate in activities and sports that you enjoy

Cons

  • Risk of injury or setback if returning to running too soon or without proper preparation
  • Cost and time commitment of rehabilitation program
  • Limitations on activity and sport participation during recovery period

Returning to running after ACL surgery can be a challenging but rewarding process. By following a structured rehabilitation program and taking the time to properly prepare, you can safely return to running and enjoy the many benefits that come with it.
