How To Do Pelvic Floor Exercises

Pelvic floor exercises are also known as Kegels. These exercises are designed to strengthen the muscles in your pelvic floor, which can help with bladder control, sexual function, and overall pelvic floor health. Here's how to do Kegels:

  1. Find the right muscles. To do Kegels, you need to contract the muscles that you use to stop yourself from urinating. You can do this by trying to stop the flow of urine when you're using the bathroom.
  2. Squeeze and hold. Once you've identified the right muscles, squeeze them and hold for three seconds. Then, relax for three seconds.
  3. Repeat. Do this exercise 10 to 15 times in a row, three times a day.

Can You Do Pelvic Floor Exercises Everyday?

The short answer is yes, you can do pelvic floor exercises every day. In fact, doing Kegels regularly is the best way to strengthen your pelvic floor muscles and improve your overall pelvic health. However, it's important to listen to your body and take breaks when you need them.

Tips for Doing Pelvic Floor Exercises Everyday

Here are some tips for doing pelvic floor exercises every day:

  • Set a reminder. It can be easy to forget to do your exercises, so set a reminder on your phone or calendar to do them at the same time every day.
  • Start slow. If you're new to pelvic floor exercises, start with just a few repetitions and gradually increase as you get stronger.
  • Breathe. Remember to breathe while doing Kegels. Don't hold your breath or tense up other muscles while you're doing the exercise.
  • Don't overdo it. If you're feeling sore or fatigued, take a break and try again later. It's important to listen to your body and not push yourself too hard.

Solution to Doing Pelvic Floor Exercises Everyday

If you're having trouble sticking to your pelvic floor exercise routine, here are some solutions to help you stay motivated:

  • Find a buddy. Ask a friend or partner to do pelvic floor exercises with you. This can help keep you accountable and make the exercises more fun.
  • Reward yourself. Set a goal and reward yourself when you reach it. For example, if you do your exercises every day for a month, treat yourself to a massage or a nice dinner.
  • Try different positions. You don't have to do Kegels lying down. You can also do them while sitting, standing, or even walking.

FAQs About Pelvic Floor Exercises

What Are the Benefits of Pelvic Floor Exercises?

Pelvic floor exercises can help with bladder control, sexual function, and overall pelvic floor health. They can also help prevent urinary incontinence and pelvic organ prolapse.

Can Men Do Pelvic Floor Exercises?

Yes, men can do pelvic floor exercises. In fact, they can help with bladder control and erectile dysfunction.

Can You Do Pelvic Floor Exercises During Pregnancy?

Yes, pelvic floor exercises are safe to do during pregnancy. In fact, they can help prepare your pelvic floor for childbirth and prevent urinary incontinence after delivery.

How Long Does It Take to See Results from Pelvic Floor Exercises?

It can take several weeks or months to see results from pelvic floor exercises. Consistency is key!

Pros and Cons of Doing Pelvic Floor Exercises Everyday

Pros

  • Improved bladder control
  • Better sexual function
  • Reduced risk of urinary incontinence and pelvic organ prolapse
  • Improved overall pelvic floor health

Cons

  • It can be difficult to remember to do the exercises every day
  • It can take time to see results
  • It's important to listen to your body and take breaks when you need them

Overall, doing pelvic floor exercises every day can be a great way to improve your pelvic floor health and overall well-being. Remember to start slow, listen to your body, and stay consistent!
