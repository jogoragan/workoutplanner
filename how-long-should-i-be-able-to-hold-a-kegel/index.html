What is a Kegel?

A kegel is an exercise that strengthens the pelvic floor muscles. These muscles support the bladder, uterus, and rectum. Kegels can be done by both men and women and are often recommended for people with urinary incontinence, pelvic organ prolapse, or sexual dysfunction.

How Long Should I Hold a Kegel?

When it comes to kegel exercises, the length of time you should hold a contraction depends on your current level of strength. If you're just starting out, aim for a hold of 3-5 seconds. As you get stronger, you can increase the length of time to 10 seconds or more. However, it's important not to overdo it. Holding a kegel for too long can cause fatigue and muscle strain.

How to Improve Your Kegel Strength

Like any other muscle, the pelvic floor muscles can be strengthened with exercise. To improve your kegel strength, try the following:

  1. Identify your pelvic floor muscles: To do this, try to stop the flow of urine midstream. The muscles you use to do this are your pelvic floor muscles.
  2. Contract your muscles: Squeeze your pelvic floor muscles, holding for the recommended length of time.
  3. Relax your muscles: Release the contraction and relax your muscles. Rest for a few seconds before repeating the exercise.

Tips for Kegel Exercises

Here are some additional tips to help you get the most out of your kegel exercises:

  • Do your kegels regularly: Aim for at least three sets of 10 repetitions per day.
  • Don't hold your breath: Breathe normally throughout the exercise.
  • Don't overdo it: Avoid doing too many kegels at once, as this can cause muscle fatigue and strain.
  • Combine with other exercises: Kegels can be done in combination with other pelvic floor exercises, such as bridges or squats.

Solutions for Weak Kegels

If you're having trouble with your kegel exercises, there are a few things you can do to help:

  • Use a kegel exerciser: These devices can help you identify and strengthen your pelvic floor muscles.
  • See a physical therapist: A physical therapist can assess your pelvic floor muscles and provide specific exercises to strengthen them.
  • Practice mindfulness: Stress and anxiety can contribute to muscle tension, so practicing relaxation techniques like deep breathing or meditation may help.

FAQ

What are the benefits of kegel exercises?

Kegel exercises can improve bladder control, reduce urinary incontinence, improve sexual function, and prevent pelvic organ prolapse.

Can men do kegel exercises?

Yes, kegel exercises can be done by both men and women.

How often should I do kegel exercises?

For best results, aim to do kegel exercises at least three times per day.
