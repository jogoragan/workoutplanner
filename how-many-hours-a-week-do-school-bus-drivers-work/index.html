How Many Hours a Week Do School Bus Drivers Work? | 2023 Guide

If you are interested in becoming a school bus driver, one of the questions you may have is how many hours a week do school bus drivers work? The answer to this question can vary depending on the school district and the specific route you drive. In this guide, we will explore the typical work hours for school bus drivers and other important information you should know.

Typical Work Hours for School Bus Drivers

In general, school bus drivers work anywhere from 20 to 40 hours per week. Most school districts require school bus drivers to work split shifts, which means driving in the morning and afternoon with a break in between. The morning shift typically starts around 6:00 or 7:00 a.m. and ends around 9:00 a.m. The afternoon shift typically starts around 2:00 or 3:00 p.m. and ends around 5:00 or 6:00 p.m.

Some school districts may also require school bus drivers to work additional hours for field trips, sporting events, and other school-related activities. These additional hours may be paid as overtime or included in the driver's regular salary.

Overtime Pay for School Bus Drivers

According to the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA), school bus drivers are typically considered non-exempt employees, which means they are entitled to overtime pay for any hours worked over 40 hours in a workweek. However, some school districts may offer alternative compensation plans that include additional pay for extra hours worked, rather than paying overtime.

Scheduling Flexibility for School Bus Drivers

One of the benefits of being a school bus driver is the scheduling flexibility. School bus drivers typically have summers off and may also have holidays and weekends off, depending on the school district. This can be a great opportunity for individuals who are looking for a part-time job or who need a flexible schedule for other obligations.

Health Benefits for School Bus Drivers

Most school districts offer health benefits for school bus drivers, including medical, dental, and vision insurance. Some districts may also offer retirement plans and other benefits.

Training Requirements for School Bus Drivers

In order to become a school bus driver, you must meet certain training requirements set by your state and school district. These requirements typically include a commercial driver's license (CDL) with a passenger endorsement, a clean driving record, and completion of a school bus driver training program. Some states may also require additional certifications or training, such as CPR and first aid certification.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much do school bus drivers get paid?

The average hourly pay for school bus drivers is around $16-$20, depending on the school district and location. Some school districts may offer additional pay for experience, certifications, or extra hours worked.

Is being a school bus driver a good job?

Being a school bus driver can be a good job for individuals who enjoy working with children and need a flexible schedule. However, it may not be the best choice for individuals who are looking for a full-time job or who do not enjoy driving or working with children.

What are the risks of being a school bus driver?

Like any job, being a school bus driver comes with certain risks. School bus drivers may be at risk for accidents, injuries, and exposure to illnesses. However, most school districts have safety protocols in place to minimize these risks.

Conclusion

If you are considering becoming a school bus driver, it is important to understand the typical work hours, overtime pay, scheduling flexibility, health benefits, and training requirements. By doing your research and understanding what to expect, you can make an informed decision about whether this is the right job for you.
