If you're a student or parent in the Anne Arundel County Public Schools (AACPS) district, you may have heard about the AB schedule. This schedule, which alternates between A and B days, has become increasingly common in high schools across the country. In this article, we'll explain what the AACPS AB schedule is, how it works, and what the pros and cons are.

What is the AACPS AB Schedule?

The AACPS AB schedule is a schedule that alternates between A and B days. On A days, students attend their A day classes, and on B days, they attend their B day classes. This means that students have different classes on different days of the week. The schedule is designed to reduce the number of students in school at any given time, which can help with social distancing and reduce the risk of spreading COVID-19.

How Does the AACPS AB Schedule Work?

The AACPS AB schedule works by dividing students into two groups, A and B. Group A attends school on A days, and Group B attends school on B days. Each group attends school in person for two days per week and learns remotely for three days per week. This allows for social distancing in the classroom and reduces the number of students in the building at any given time.

How Do You Know Which Days are A Days and Which Days are B Days?

The AACPS AB schedule is typically posted on the school's website and communicated to students and parents through email or other means. It's important to pay attention to which days are A days and which days are B days, as this will determine which classes you attend on which days.

What are the Pros of the AACPS AB Schedule?

There are several pros to the AACPS AB schedule. First, it allows for social distancing in the classroom, which can reduce the risk of spreading COVID-19. Second, it can help students stay organized and focused, as they only have to worry about a few classes at a time. Finally, it can provide more one-on-one time with teachers, as there are fewer students in the classroom.

What are the Cons of the AACPS AB Schedule?

There are also some cons to the AACPS AB schedule. One is that students may have difficulty keeping track of which classes they have on which days. Another is that students may miss out on opportunities to interact with their peers and participate in extracurricular activities. Finally, students may find it difficult to adjust to the alternating schedule and may struggle to stay motivated.

Tips for Succeeding on the AACPS AB Schedule

If you're a student on the AACPS AB schedule, there are several tips you can follow to help you succeed. First, make sure to keep track of which classes you have on which days. You may want to create a schedule or use a planner to help you stay organized. Second, take advantage of the one-on-one time with teachers by asking questions and getting extra help when you need it. Finally, try to stay motivated by setting goals and staying focused on your long-term objectives.
