Do You Lose Muscle When Doing Cardio? Find Out Here

Cardio exercises such as running, cycling, and swimming are great ways to improve your cardiovascular health and burn calories. However, many people fear that doing cardio may cause them to lose muscle mass. In this article, we will explore the truth behind this myth and provide you with tips on how to maintain your muscle while doing cardio.

How Does Cardio Affect Muscle Mass?

Cardio exercises primarily target the cardiovascular system and help to improve your heart and lung function. While doing cardio, your body burns calories, and if you are in a calorie deficit, you may lose weight. This weight loss may include both fat and muscle mass.

However, losing muscle mass while doing cardio is not inevitable. It depends on several factors such as the type and intensity of cardio exercise, nutrition, and rest. High-intensity cardio exercises such as sprinting and HIIT may cause more muscle loss than low-intensity exercises such as walking or light cycling.

How to Maintain Your Muscle Mass While Doing Cardio

If you want to maintain your muscle mass while doing cardio, here are some tips to follow:

1. Resistance Training

Incorporating resistance training into your workout routine can help you maintain your muscle mass. Strength training exercises such as weightlifting and bodyweight exercises help to build and maintain muscle mass.

2. Fuel Your Body

Eating a balanced diet that is rich in protein can help to maintain your muscle mass. Protein is essential for muscle growth and repair. Aim to consume at least 1 gram of protein per pound of body weight per day.

3. Rest and Recovery

Rest and recovery are essential for muscle growth and repair. Make sure to give your body enough rest between workouts and get enough sleep to allow your muscles to recover.

4. Low-Intensity Cardio

Low-intensity cardio exercises such as walking, light cycling, and swimming can help to burn calories without causing muscle loss. These exercises are also great for recovery days.

FAQ

Do You Lose Muscle When Doing Cardio?

It depends on several factors such as the type and intensity of cardio exercise, nutrition, and rest. High-intensity cardio exercises may cause more muscle loss than low-intensity exercises.

Can You Build Muscle While Doing Cardio?

It is possible to build muscle while doing cardio, but it depends on the type of cardio exercise, nutrition, and rest. Resistance training is more effective for building muscle mass.

How Much Cardio Should You Do?

The amount of cardio you should do depends on your fitness goals and level of fitness. Aim to do at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio or 75 minutes of high-intensity cardio per week.

Pros and Cons of Doing Cardio

Pros

  • Improves cardiovascular health
  • Burns calories
  • Reduces the risk of chronic diseases
  • Boosts mood and energy levels

Cons

  • May cause muscle loss if not done correctly
  • May lead to overuse injuries
  • Can be time-consuming
  • May not be effective for weight loss alone

Overall, doing cardio is an excellent way to improve your overall health and fitness. However, it is essential to do it correctly to avoid muscle loss and injuries. Incorporating resistance training, eating a balanced diet, and getting enough rest can help you maintain your muscle mass while doing cardio.
