Welcome to our guide on the best things to do on Mother's Day in London. We've put together a list of activities that will help you enjoy the day with your mother or grandmother. Whether you're looking for a relaxing afternoon tea, a fun day out, or a unique experience, we've got you covered.

Afternoon Tea

One of the most popular things to do on Mother's Day in London is to treat your mother to a traditional afternoon tea. There are many options to choose from, ranging from classic tea rooms to luxury hotels.

The Ritz London is one of the most famous places to have afternoon tea in London. The elegant surroundings and traditional service create a truly memorable experience. Another great option is the iconic Claridge's Hotel, which offers a range of teas and pastries in their beautiful Art Deco surroundings.

Shopping

London is a shopper's paradise, and Mother's Day is the perfect excuse to indulge in some retail therapy. From luxury department stores to independent boutiques, there's something for everyone.

One of the most popular shopping destinations in London is Oxford Street, which is home to over 300 shops. Another great option is Covent Garden, which has a mix of high street stores and independent boutiques in a charming pedestrianised area.

Sightseeing

If your mother is a fan of history, art or culture, then London has plenty of sights to see. From the iconic landmarks like the Tower of London and Buckingham Palace to the world-famous museums like the British Museum and the National Gallery, there's something for everyone.

If you're looking for something a little more off the beaten track, then why not take a walk along the South Bank and enjoy the street performers and views of the Thames?

Pampering

If you're looking to treat your mother to some well-deserved pampering, then London has plenty of options to choose from. From luxurious spas to beauty treatments, there's something for everyone.

The Four Seasons Spa in Mayfair is a great choice for a relaxing spa day, with a range of treatments and therapies available. Alternatively, you could book a manicure or pedicure at one of the many nail salons in London.

Unique Experiences

If you're looking for something a little more unusual to do on Mother's Day, then London has plenty of unique experiences to offer. From afternoon tea on a bus to a guided tour of the city's street art, there's something for everyone.

One of the most popular unique experiences in London is the London Eye, which offers stunning views of the city from 135 metres in the air. Alternatively, you could book a tour of the Houses of Parliament or take a trip on a vintage Routemaster bus.

Tips for a Great Mother's Day

  • Book in advance to avoid disappointment
  • Ask your mother what she would like to do
  • Don't overschedule - leave time for relaxation
  • Make sure to take plenty of photos to remember the day
  • Remember that it's the thought that counts - it doesn't have to be expensive or extravagant

FAQs

What is the best thing to do on Mother's Day in London?

It depends on your mother's preferences, but some popular options include afternoon tea, shopping, sightseeing, and pampering.

Is it necessary to book in advance?

Yes, it's always best to book in advance to avoid disappointment, especially for popular activities like afternoon tea and spa treatments.

What should I wear for afternoon tea?

Afternoon tea is a formal occasion, so it's best to dress smartly. Men should wear a suit or smart trousers and a shirt, and women should wear a dress or smart trousers and a blouse.
