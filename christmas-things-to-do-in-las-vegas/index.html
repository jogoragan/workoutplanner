Welcome to Las Vegas, one of the most exciting and vibrant cities in the world! Christmas is just around the corner, and there’s no better way to celebrate it than to visit Las Vegas. Whether you’re traveling with family, friends, or as a couple, there are plenty of Christmas things to do in Las Vegas. In this article, we’ll explore some of the best activities and events to make your Christmas in Las Vegas a memorable one.

1. Ice Skating at the Cosmopolitan

Ice skating is one of the most popular Christmas activities, and the Cosmopolitan of Las Vegas offers a unique ice skating experience. The Ice Rink at the Cosmopolitan is located on the rooftop, offering stunning views of the Las Vegas skyline. The rink is open from November to January and features fire pits, s'mores, and a full bar. If you're not a fan of ice skating, you can still enjoy the atmosphere while sipping on a hot chocolate or a winter cocktail.

2. Christmas at the Bellagio

The Bellagio is known for its stunning fountain show, but during Christmas, it transforms into a winter wonderland. The Conservatory and Botanical Gardens are decorated with thousands of flowers, plants, and lights. The main attraction is the 42-foot-tall Christmas tree, adorned with ornaments and lights. The Bellagio also offers holiday-themed shows, including Cirque du Soleil's "O" and the Bellagio Fountains Holiday Spectacular.

3. Magical Forest at Opportunity Village

The Magical Forest at Opportunity Village is a must-visit Christmas attraction for families. The forest features millions of lights, a train ride, an ice skating rink, and a toboggan slide. There are also food vendors, live entertainment, and visits from Santa Claus. The best part is that all proceeds go to Opportunity Village, a local non-profit organization that provides services to people with intellectual disabilities.

4. Holiday Cactus Garden at Ethel M Chocolates

If you're looking for a unique Christmas attraction, head over to Ethel M Chocolates. The Holiday Cactus Garden features over 1 million lights, cacti, and other desert plants. There's also a chocolate factory tour, where you can see how the chocolates are made and sample some of the delicious treats. Admission to the garden is free, and the factory tour is $15 per person.

5. Santa's Wonderland at Bass Pro Shops

For a fun and free Christmas activity, visit Santa's Wonderland at Bass Pro Shops. The wonderland features a Christmas village, a train ride, and a free photo with Santa Claus. There are also crafts and games for kids, and a chance to write a letter to Santa. The wonderland is open from November to December, and admission is free.

6. The Nutcracker at the Smith Center

The Nutcracker is a classic Christmas ballet, and the Smith Center offers a spectacular performance. The ballet tells the story of a young girl's magical Christmas journey, featuring beautiful costumes, sets, and music. The Nutcracker is performed by the Nevada Ballet Theatre, and tickets range from $29 to $179.

7. Holiday Lights Tour

If you want to see the best Christmas lights in Las Vegas, take a holiday lights tour. The tour takes you to some of the most decorated neighborhoods, including the famous "Christmas Street" in Henderson. You'll also see the lights at the Las Vegas Motor Speedway and the Glittering Lights at the Las Vegas Speedway. The tour lasts about 3 hours, and tickets range from $60 to $120.

8. Christmas Shopping

Las Vegas is known for its shopping, and Christmas is the perfect time to indulge in some retail therapy. The Forum Shops at Caesars Palace offers a wide range of luxury brands, while the Miracle Mile Shops at Planet Hollywood has more affordable options. You can also visit the Grand Canal Shoppes at the Venetian, which offers a unique shopping experience with its indoor canals and gondola rides.

9. New Year's Eve Celebration

New Year's Eve in Las Vegas is one of the biggest celebrations in the world. The Strip is closed to traffic, and thousands of people gather to watch the fireworks and countdown to midnight. There are also concerts, parties, and shows throughout the city. If you're planning to celebrate New Year's Eve in Las Vegas, make sure to book your accommodations well in advance.

10. Conclusion

Las Vegas is a city that never sleeps, and Christmas is no exception. From ice skating to holiday lights tours, there are plenty of Christmas things to do in Las Vegas. Whether you're looking for family-friendly activities or romantic date ideas, Las Vegas has something for everyone. So, pack your bags, and come celebrate Christmas in Las Vegas!
