Welcome to our guide on what a newborn should do during the day! As a new parent, it can be overwhelming to figure out what your baby needs throughout the day. In this article, we'll discuss the different activities and routines that your newborn should follow during the day to keep them healthy, happy, and well-rested.

Sleep Schedule

Newborns need a lot of sleep, so it's important to establish a sleep schedule early on. Most newborns sleep for about 16-17 hours a day, but they don't sleep for longer than 3-4 hours at a time. This means that you'll need to wake up your baby for feedings throughout the day and night.

During the day, your baby should take several naps to ensure that they get enough rest. It's important to create a consistent sleep environment for your baby, such as a dark and quiet room with a comfortable temperature. You can also establish a sleep routine, such as swaddling your baby, singing a lullaby, or reading a story before bedtime.

Feeding Schedule

Newborns need to be fed frequently, as they have small stomachs and can't go for long periods without food. Most newborns need to be fed every 2-3 hours, or whenever they show signs of hunger, such as rooting or sucking on their hands.

You can either breastfeed or bottle-feed your baby, depending on your preference. Breastfeeding is recommended as it provides essential nutrients and helps strengthen your baby's immune system. If you choose to bottle-feed, make sure to use a formula that is appropriate for your baby's age and needs.

Playtime Schedule

While newborns spend most of their time sleeping and eating, they also need some playtime to stimulate their senses and develop their motor skills. You can engage your baby in simple activities such as talking to them, making eye contact, and showing them different objects and textures.

You can also use toys such as rattles, mobiles, and soft blocks to encourage your baby's development. Make sure to choose toys that are safe and appropriate for your baby's age and abilities.

Tummy Time

Tummy time is an important activity that helps your baby develop their muscles and improve their coordination. It's recommended to start tummy time early on, even from the first day home from the hospital.

You can start with short periods of tummy time, gradually increasing the time as your baby gets stronger. Make sure to supervise your baby during tummy time and place them on a soft and safe surface.

Bath Time

Bathing your newborn can be a fun and bonding experience for both you and your baby. However, it's important to be cautious and follow safety guidelines to prevent accidents and injuries.

You can give your baby a bath in a baby bathtub or a sink, using warm water and a mild baby soap. Make sure to support your baby's head and neck at all times, and never leave them unattended in the water.

Outdoor Activities

While it's important to keep your newborn safe and protected from the elements, spending time outdoors can be beneficial for their health and development. You can take your baby for a walk in a stroller or carrier, or simply sit outside and enjoy some fresh air and sunshine.

Make sure to dress your baby appropriately for the weather, and avoid direct sunlight or extreme temperatures. You can also introduce your baby to nature by showing them flowers, trees, and animals.

Reading Time

Reading to your baby is a great way to promote language development and cognitive skills. You can start reading to your baby from a young age, using simple and colorful books with large pictures.

You can also use different voices and sounds to make the reading experience more interactive and engaging for your baby. Make sure to choose age-appropriate books and avoid overstimulation.

Socializing

While newborns don't have a lot of social skills yet, they still benefit from interacting with their parents and other people. You can talk to your baby, sing to them, and make eye contact to help them feel loved and connected.

You can also introduce your baby to new people and environments, such as family members, friends, and public places. However, make sure to follow safety guidelines and avoid exposing your baby to germs and viruses.

Safety Tips

Keeping your newborn safe is a top priority, so it's important to follow safety guidelines and precautions. Some tips for keeping your baby safe include:

  • Always supervise your baby, especially during feeding, bathing, and tummy time
  • Use a car seat whenever you travel with your baby
  • Keep your baby's sleeping area free of loose bedding, toys, and other objects
  • Make sure your baby's crib meets safety standards and is placed away from windows, blinds, and cords
  • Keep harmful substances and objects out of reach of your baby

FAQs

How much sleep does a newborn need?

Newborns need about 16-17 hours of sleep a day, but they don't sleep for longer than 3-4 hours at a time.

How often should a newborn be fed?

Newborns need to be fed every 2-3 hours, or whenever they show signs of hunger.

When should I start tummy time with my baby?

You can start tummy time from the first day home from the hospital, but make sure to start with short periods and gradually increase the time as your baby gets stronger.

What toys are safe for newborns?

Choose toys that are safe, non-toxic, and appropriate for your baby's age and abilities. Soft toys, rattles, and mobiles are good options for newborns.

How can I keep my newborn safe?

Follow safety guidelines and precautions, such as supervising your baby at all times, using a car seat, keeping harmful objects out of reach, and creating a safe sleeping environment.

We hope this guide has been helpful in giving you an idea of what your newborn should do during the day. Remember to be patient, attentive, and loving with your baby, and enjoy this special time together!
