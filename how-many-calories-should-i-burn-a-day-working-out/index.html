How Many Calories Should I Burn A Day Working Out

Are you wondering how many calories you should burn a day working out? If so, you're not alone. Many people struggle with finding the right balance between exercise and calorie burn. The truth is, the number of calories you should burn a day working out depends on a variety of factors, including your age, weight, height, and activity level.

Description

Burning calories is an essential part of staying healthy and maintaining a healthy weight. Exercise is one of the best ways to burn calories, but it can be challenging to know how many calories you should be burning each day. In this article, we will discuss how many calories you should aim to burn each day when working out, as well as tips for achieving your daily calorie burn goals.

How to Burn Calories

Before we dive into how many calories you should be burning each day, let's first discuss how to burn calories. There are many ways to burn calories, including:

  • Cardiovascular exercise, such as running, cycling, or swimming
  • Strength training, such as weightlifting or resistance band exercises
  • High-intensity interval training (HIIT)
  • Low-intensity steady-state (LISS) exercise, such as walking or yoga

Regardless of the type of exercise you choose, the key to burning calories is to get your heart rate up and keep it up for an extended period. Aim for at least 30 minutes of exercise per day, and gradually increase the duration and intensity as your fitness level improves.

Step by Step: How to Calculate How Many Calories You Should Burn

Now that we've covered how to burn calories let's discuss how to calculate how many calories you should be burning each day. Here's a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Determine Your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)

Your BMR is the number of calories your body burns at rest. You can calculate your BMR using an online calculator or by using the following formula:

BMR = 10 x weight (kg) + 6.25 x height (cm) - 5 x age (years) + 5

Step 2: Determine Your Activity Level

Your activity level will determine how many calories you burn each day. Use the following chart to determine your activity level:

  • Sedentary (little or no exercise): BMR x 1.2
  • Lightly active (light exercise 1-3 days per week): BMR x 1.375
  • Moderately active (moderate exercise 3-5 days per week): BMR x 1.55
  • Very active (hard exercise 6-7 days per week): BMR x 1.725
  • Super active (very hard exercise, physical job, training 2x per day): BMR x 1.9

Step 3: Determine Your Calorie Burn Goal

Once you have determined your BMR and activity level, you can calculate your daily calorie burn goal by multiplying your BMR by your activity level. For example, if your BMR is 1500, and you are moderately active, your daily calorie burn goal would be 2325 (1500 x 1.55).

Tips for Achieving Your Daily Calorie Burn Goal

Now that you know how many calories you should be burning each day, let's discuss some tips for achieving your daily calorie burn goal:

  • Find an exercise routine that you enjoy and stick to it.
  • Gradually increase the duration and intensity of your workouts to avoid injury.
  • Incorporate strength training into your routine to build muscle and boost your metabolism.
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet to fuel your workouts and aid in muscle recovery.
  • Get plenty of rest and recovery time to allow your body to repair and rebuild.

FAQ

What happens if I don't burn enough calories?

If you don't burn enough calories, you may not achieve your weight loss or fitness goals. To lose weight, you need to create a calorie deficit by burning more calories than you consume. If you're not burning enough calories, you may need to adjust your diet or exercise routine to achieve your goals.

Can I burn too many calories?

Yes, it is possible to burn too many calories. If you're burning more calories than you're consuming, you may experience fatigue, muscle loss, and other health issues. It's important to find the right balance between calorie burn and calorie intake to achieve your goals safely and effectively.

Pros and Cons

Pros

  • Burning calories through exercise can help you lose weight and improve your overall health.
  • Knowing how many calories you should be burning each day can help you set realistic goals and track your progress.
  • There are many types of exercise to choose from, so you can find an activity that you enjoy and stick to it.

Cons

  • Calculating how many calories you should be burning each day can be confusing and time-consuming.
  • If you're not used to exercising, it can be challenging to find the motivation to get started.
  • If you overdo it with exercise, you may experience fatigue, muscle loss, and other health issues.

Overall, knowing how many calories you should be burning each day can help you achieve your weight loss and fitness goals. By finding an exercise routine that you enjoy and gradually increasing the duration and intensity, you can burn calories and improve your health over time.
