Have you ever found yourself confused about what time of day it is? Are you unsure whether it's morning, afternoon, or evening? The answer lies in understanding the difference between AM and PM. In this article, we'll explain which is morning PM or AM and provide some helpful tips to keep you on track.

What Does AM Mean?

The acronym AM stands for ante meridiem. In Latin, ante meridiem translates to "before midday." This means that any time between midnight and noon is considered AM. For example, 7:00 AM is seven hours after midnight and seven hours before noon.

What Does PM Mean?

The acronym PM stands for post meridiem. In Latin, post meridiem translates to "after midday." This means that any time between noon and midnight is considered PM. For example, 7:00 PM is seven hours after noon and seven hours before midnight.

How to Keep Track of AM and PM

Keeping track of AM and PM is essential, especially when scheduling appointments or meetings. Here are some helpful tips:

1. Use a 24-Hour Clock

A 24-hour clock, also known as military time, eliminates any confusion about AM and PM. Instead of using a.m. or p.m., the time is displayed in a 24-hour format. For example, 7:00 AM would be 07:00, and 7:00 PM would be 19:00.

2. Say the Time Out Loud

If you're unsure whether it's morning or evening, say the time out loud. This will help you remember whether it's AM or PM. For example, saying "it's seven in the morning" or "it's seven in the evening" can help clarify the time of day.

3. Pay Attention to the Sun

During the day, the position of the sun can help you determine whether it's AM or PM. In the morning, the sun is rising, and in the evening, the sun is setting. If you're indoors or can't see the sun, pay attention to the lighting. Bright sunlight typically indicates morning, while dimmer lighting suggests evening.

FAQ

What Does Noon Mean?

Noon is the exact midpoint of the day. It's 12:00 PM or 12:00 noon.

What Does Midnight Mean?

Midnight is the exact midpoint of the night. It's 12:00 AM or 00:00.

Pros and Cons of AM and PM

Pros

Understanding AM and PM is essential for scheduling appointments and meetings.

A 24-hour clock eliminates any confusion about AM and PM.

Cons

Using AM and PM can be confusing, especially for non-native English speakers.

Accidentally scheduling a morning appointment for the evening can be frustrating and time-consuming.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the difference between AM and PM is simple: AM is before noon, and PM is after noon. Remembering this can be challenging, but using a 24-hour clock, saying the time out loud, and paying attention to the sun can all help. By following these tips, you'll never have to wonder which is morning PM or AM again!
