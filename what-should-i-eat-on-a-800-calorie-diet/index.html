If you're considering an 800 calorie diet, it's important to know what to eat to ensure you're getting the nutrients you need while still achieving your weight loss goals. In this article, we'll break down the types of foods you should focus on and provide some helpful tips and meal ideas to get you started.

How to Start Your 800 Calorie Diet

Before jumping into an 800 calorie diet, it's important to speak with a healthcare professional to ensure it's safe for you. Once you have the green light, you'll want to focus on nutrient-dense foods that will keep you feeling full and satisfied throughout the day.

Step 1: Focus on Protein and Fiber

Protein and fiber are two essential components of a healthy, balanced diet. Not only do they keep you feeling full, but they also provide essential nutrients and energy to keep you going throughout the day. Some high-protein options include:

  • Lean meats like chicken, turkey, and fish
  • Eggs
  • Beans and legumes
  • Nuts and seeds

Fiber-rich foods include:

  • Fruits like berries, bananas, and apples
  • Veggies like broccoli, spinach, and kale
  • Whole grains like quinoa, brown rice, and oats
  • Legumes like lentils and black beans

Step 2: Cut Back on Carbs and Fats

While carbs and fats are important for a healthy diet, they can quickly add up in calories. To stay within your 800 calorie limit, it's important to limit your intake of these foods. Some low-carb options include:

  • Leafy greens like spinach and kale
  • Cruciferous veggies like broccoli and cauliflower
  • Berries like strawberries and raspberries
  • Lean protein sources like chicken and fish

When it comes to fats, focus on healthy options like:

  • Avocado
  • Nuts and seeds
  • Olive oil
  • Fatty fish like salmon

Tips for Success on an 800 Calorie Diet

While an 800 calorie diet can be effective for weight loss, it's important to do it in a healthy and sustainable way. Here are some tips to help you succeed:

  • Drink plenty of water throughout the day to stay hydrated
  • Plan your meals ahead of time to ensure you're getting all the nutrients you need
  • Include a variety of fruits and vegetables to ensure you're getting a range of vitamins and minerals
  • Don't skip meals - this can make you more likely to overeat later in the day
  • Consider working with a registered dietitian to ensure you're meeting your nutritional needs

Meal Ideas for an 800 Calorie Diet

Here are some meal ideas to help you get started:

  • Breakfast: Scrambled eggs with spinach and mushrooms, topped with salsa and served with a side of berries
  • Lunch: Grilled chicken breast with a side of roasted veggies and quinoa
  • Dinner: Baked salmon with a side of steamed broccoli and brown rice
  • Snack: Apple slices with almond butter

FAQ

Is an 800 calorie diet safe?

An 800 calorie diet can be safe if done correctly and under the guidance of a healthcare professional. However, it's important to ensure you're getting all the nutrients your body needs and not going too low in calories.

How much weight can I expect to lose on an 800 calorie diet?

The amount of weight you can expect to lose on an 800 calorie diet will vary depending on a number of factors, including your starting weight, activity level, and metabolism. However, it's not uncommon to lose 1-2 pounds per week on a diet of this calorie intake.

How long should I stay on an 800 calorie diet?

The length of time you should stay on an 800 calorie diet will depend on your individual weight loss goals and the guidance of your healthcare professional. It's important to not stay on a low-calorie diet for too long, as this can have negative consequences on your health.

Pros and Cons of an 800 Calorie Diet

Pros:

  • Can lead to rapid weight loss
  • May improve insulin sensitivity
  • Can help jumpstart a healthy lifestyle

Cons:

  • May not provide enough nutrients for long-term health
  • Can slow metabolism over time
  • May lead to binge eating or other disordered eating behaviors

Overall, an 800 calorie diet can be an effective way to jumpstart weight loss and improve overall health. However, it's important to do it in a healthy and sustainable way and under the guidance of a healthcare professional.
