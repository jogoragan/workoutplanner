Give Up Phrasal Verb Meaning And Sentence

Written by Bobby Mar 01, 2023 · 3 min read

.

Description

Give up is a phrasal verb that means to stop doing something or to surrender. This phrasal verb is commonly used in everyday English language and can be used in different contexts, such as giving up on a project, giving up a bad habit, or giving up during a competition.

How to Use Give Up Phrasal Verb

To use give up, you need to combine the verb 'give' with the preposition 'up'. This creates a new meaning altogether. The phrasal verb can be used in different tenses and forms, depending on the context. For example: - She gave up smoking two years ago. (past tense) - I am giving up on this project because it's too difficult. (present continuous tense) - They will give up the fight once they realize they can't win. (future tense)

Step by Step Guide

1. Identify the context where you want to use give up. 2. Combine the verb 'give' with the preposition 'up'. 3. Use the phrasal verb in the appropriate tense and form. 4. Make sure the sentence makes sense and conveys the intended meaning.

Tips

- Use give up to express surrender, quitting, or abandoning. - Be careful with the context as give up can have different meanings depending on the situation. - Practice using give up in different tenses and forms to become more familiar with it. - Use synonyms or other phrasal verbs to avoid repetition in writing or speaking.
