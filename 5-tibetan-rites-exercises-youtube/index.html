1. What are the 5 Tibetan Rites Exercises?

The 5 Tibetan Rites Exercises, also known as the "Fountain of Youth," are a set of yoga exercises that originated from Tibet. These exercises are believed to have been practiced by Tibetan monks who lived for more than 100 years. The exercises are designed to promote physical, mental, and spiritual well-being.

The 5 Tibetan Rites Exercises consist of five postures that are performed in a specific sequence. These exercises are simple and can be performed by anyone, regardless of age or fitness level. The exercises are also known to be energizing and can help to improve your overall health.

2. How to Perform the 5 Tibetan Rites Exercises?

Below are the steps to perform the 5 Tibetan Rites Exercises:

Step 1: Spin

Stand with your arms outstretched and parallel to the ground. Spin clockwise until you become slightly dizzy. Gradually increase the number of spins as you progress.

Step 2: Leg Raises

Lie on your back with your arms at your sides. Raise your legs to a 90-degree angle without bending your knees. Gradually increase the number of leg raises as you progress.

Step 3: Camel Pose

Kneel on the ground with your hands on your hips. Arch your back and lean back as far as you can. Hold the pose for a few seconds before returning to the starting position.

Step 4: Tabletop Pose

Sit on the ground with your legs straight out in front of you. Place your hands on the ground behind you with your fingers pointing towards your feet. Lift your hips and torso until your body is parallel to the ground.

Step 5: Downward Dog

Assume a push-up position with your hands and feet on the ground. Raise your hips towards the ceiling to form a triangle shape with your body. Hold the pose for a few seconds before returning to the starting position.
