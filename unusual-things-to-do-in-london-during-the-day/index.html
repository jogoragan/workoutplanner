Welcome to London, the city that never sleeps and always has something new to offer! If you're looking for something different to do during the day, we've got you covered. We've compiled a list of the most unusual things to do in London during the day. From exploring hidden gems to trying out new experiences, there's something for everyone.

Explore Hidden Gems

London is full of hidden gems waiting to be discovered. Take a walk through the streets of Covent Garden and find Neal's Yard, a small courtyard filled with colorful buildings and independent shops. Or visit the Leake Street Tunnel, an underground tunnel covered in graffiti art. Don't forget to explore Camden Passage, a hidden alleyway filled with antique shops and cafes.

Discover Street Art

If you're a fan of street art, London is the place to be. Take a tour around Shoreditch and Brick Lane to see some of the best street art in London. Or visit the Tate Modern and see the famous Banksy mural, "Love is in the Air." You can even take a street art workshop and create your own masterpiece.

Indulge in Afternoon Tea

Afternoon tea is a traditional British experience that you can't miss. Head over to Fortnum & Mason or The Ritz for a luxurious afternoon tea experience. Or try a more quirky option like the Mad Hatter's Afternoon Tea at the Sanderson Hotel, where you'll be transported to Wonderland.

Find Secret Bars

London is full of secret bars waiting to be discovered. Head over to The Mayor of Scaredy Cat Town and enter through a refrigerator door in a diner. Or visit Evans & Peel Detective Agency and give them a secret password to enter. You can even find a secret bar in a phone booth at The London Cocktail Club.

Experience Virtual Reality

Step into the future and experience virtual reality in London. Head over to The VOID in Westfield Stratford City and immerse yourself in a virtual world. Or visit Otherworld in Haggerston and try out their VR pods. You can even try VR gaming at DNA VR in Camden.

Try an Escape Room

Escape rooms are a fun and challenging experience for groups. Try out the Sherlock Holmes Escape Room at Madame Tussauds or the Escape Room at The Crystal Maze. You can even try a zombie-themed escape room at Trapped Escape Rooms.

Visit Quirky Museums

London is home to some of the quirkiest museums in the world. Visit the Viktor Wynd Museum of Curiosities, Fine Art & Natural History to see oddities like a two-headed kitten and a jar of moles. Or visit the Museum of Brands, Packaging and Advertising to see how brands have evolved over time. You can even visit the Old Operating Theatre Museum and learn about the history of surgery.

Go Outdoor Swimming

Take a dip in one of London's outdoor swimming pools. Visit the Hampstead Heath Bathing Ponds for a natural swimming experience. Or visit the Serpentine Lido in Hyde Park for a more traditional swimming experience. You can even swim in a heated rooftop pool at the Queen Elizabeth Olympic Park.

Explore Food Markets

London is a foodie's paradise, and the food markets are a must-visit. Visit Borough Market for fresh produce and artisanal foods. Or head over to Camden Market for international street food. You can even visit Maltby Street Market for unique food and drink options.

Relax in Parks

London is home to some of the most beautiful parks in the world. Visit Hyde Park for a scenic walk and a picnic. Or visit Regent's Park for a view of the London skyline. You can even visit Greenwich Park for a view of the Royal Observatory.

Tips for Exploring London

London is a big city, so it's important to plan ahead. Make sure to research the places you want to visit and check their opening times. Don't forget to wear comfortable shoes as you'll be doing a lot of walking. And always be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas.

FAQs

Q: Are the outdoor swimming pools open all year round?
A: No, the outdoor swimming pools are only open during the summer months.

Q: Do I need to book in advance for afternoon tea?
A: It's recommended to book in advance for afternoon tea, especially for popular places like The Ritz.

Pros and Cons of Unusual Things to Do in London During the Day

Pros: You'll have unique experiences and create unforgettable memories. You'll also discover hidden gems that you wouldn't have found otherwise.

Cons: Some of the activities can be expensive, and not all of them are accessible for everyone. Some of the places can also be crowded, especially during peak tourist season.

Now that you know some of the most unusual things to do in London during the day, it's time to start exploring! Whether you're a local or a tourist, there's always something new to discover in this vibrant city.
